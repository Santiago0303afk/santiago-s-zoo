Sea Lion

Sea lions are marine mammals that belong to the pinniped family. They are known for their external ear flaps, strong front flippers, and the ability to "walk" on land by rotating their hind flippers forward. Found in coastal waters, they are social creatures that often gather in large colonies. Sea lions are excellent swimmers, using their powerful flippers to navigate through the ocean with ease. Their diet mainly consists of fish and squid, and they play an essential role in maintaining the balance of marine ecosystems.
